THE RECIPES

Traditional Chinese medicine is largely unproven by modern science, but TCM users and practitioners still believe in the value of these centuries-old doctrines, compiled over many generations.

According to these doctrines, pangolin scales are used for four broad purposes: boosting lactation, treating furuncles and sores, rheumatoid arthritis, and for detoxification.

But the same doctrines also teach recipes that perform the same function as pangolin scales. Here are a few no-pangolin recipes, according to TCM doctrine.

Recipes by TCM practitioner Dr. Kong Why Hong

Please note: These ingredients are usually consumed as part of a TCM recipe. Do not self-prescribe TCM treatments, and consult a certified TCM practitioner instead. These recipes should not be seen as an endorsement of TCM over other forms of medicine.

Recipe to Boost Lactation

In Chinese medicine, it is believed that the act of giving birth will deplete the levels of Qi and blood in the mother’s body. This herbal recipe aims to boost milk supply by helping your body regain healthy levels of blood and Qi.

• Maral Roots (Rhaponticum uniflorum)

• Corn Silk (Zea mays)

• Vaccaria Seeds (Vaccaria segetalis)

• Sweetgum Fruit (Fructus liquidamberis)

• Mulberry Mistletoe (Viscum coloratum)

Recipe to Treat Furuncle and Sores

Furuncles and sores are believed to be caused by dampness and heat invading the body, and these ingredients are believed to dissipate heat and detoxify the body.

• Dragon’s Blood Tree Resin (Resina draconis)

• Common Gecko (Hemidactylus frenatus)

• Chinese Ginseng (Panax notoginseng)

• Frankincense (Boswellia sacra resin)

• Myrrh (Resina myrrhae)

• Paris Root (Paris polyphylla)

• Catechu (Acacia catechu)

• Cabardine Deer Musk (Moschus berezovskii flerov)

Recipe to Treat Rheumatoid Arthritis

Rheumatoid arthritis is a common autoimmune condition that causes inflammation, swelling, and joint pain. These ingredients are recommended in treating this condition as they are believed to exert anti-inflammatory and anti-arthritic effects.

• Gu Sui Bu (Davallia mariesii)

• Psoralea Fruit (Psoralea corylifolia)

• Rehmannia (Rehmannia glutinosa)

• Barrenwort (Epimedii herba)

• Antler Gelatin (Cornus cervi colla)

• Notopterygium Root (Notopterygium incisum)

• Gouji (Rhizoma ciboti)

• Teasel Root (Dipsacus asperoides)

• Doubletooth Pubescent Angelica Root (Angelica pubescens)

• Eucommia Bark (Eucommia ulmoides)

• Chinese Yam (Dioscorea polystachya)

• Angelica Sinensis (Radix angelicae sinensis)

• Szechuan Lovage Root (Ligusticum chuanxiong hort)

• White Peony Root (Radix paeoniae alba)

• Mormon Tea Herb (Ephedra nevadensis)

• Dried Ginger (Zingiber officinale)

• Clematis Roots (Clematis chinensis)

• Siler Root (Saposhnikovia divaricata)

• White Silkworm (Bombyx mori)

• Raw Glutinous Rice (Oryza sativa)

• Gui Zhi (Ramulus cinnamomi)

• Baizhu (Atractylodes macrocephala)

• Milkvetch Root (Astragalus)

• Fangji (Aristolochia fangchi)

• Chinese Licorice (Glycyrrhiza uralensis)

• Niuxi (Achyranthes bidentata)

Recipe For Detoxification

Traditional Chinese practitioners believe that when the body’s natural detox channels are weakened, it is important to revitalise them through resting, eliminating toxins, and nourishing the body inside out. Here are some herbal ingredients that can help with the body’s natural cleansing process.

• Goldthread Root (Coptis chinensis)

• Ox Bezoars (Calculus bovis)

• Realgar (Arsenic sulfide)

• Gypsum (Hydrated calcium sulfate)

• Rhubarb (Rheum rhabarbarum)

• Chinese Skullcap (Scutellaria baicalensis georgi)
